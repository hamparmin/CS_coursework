MNIST Digits¶

I chose an 80:20 split of training vs test data, to provide enough for my k-Nearest Neighbor model to train on and ensure good performance on the test. I used a 6-fold cross validation (6 because it split the training data evenly) to find the best parameter value $k$.

# turn the data in a (samples, feature) matrix:
n_samples = len(digits.images)
data = digits.images.reshape((n_samples, -1))

# Split data into train and test subsets (80/20 split)
X_train, X_test, y_train, y_test = train_test_split(
    data, digits.target, test_size=0.2, shuffle=False)

Interestingly, the cross-validation showed us that the best $k$ is 1. This suggests that our data points for the digits 1 and 0 are far apart enough (very different), that a single neighbor proves to be a reliable predictor.
